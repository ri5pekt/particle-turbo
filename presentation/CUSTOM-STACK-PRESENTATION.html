Technology 1: Nuxt 3 + Vue 3

Nuxt is the main website application layer. It is built on Vue, a modern frontend framework for interactive customer experiences.

  • Renders fast public pages for SEO and performance.
  • Builds product pages, landing pages, cart, checkout, account, and post-purchase pages.
  • Combines data from Medusa and Strapi before showing it to the customer.
  • Controls URL structure, redirects, SEO metadata, and caching strategy.
  • Can show server-rendered pages to Google while still supporting rich interactive components for shoppers.
  • Lets us keep cart, checkout, account, and post-purchase flows inside one consistent customer experience.
Why It Matters

Nuxt lets us keep the website fast without giving up control of the customer journey.

It is especially valuable for SEO-heavy ecommerce, landing pages, product pages, and campaign traffic.

Technology 2: Medusa Commerce

Commerce Engine

Medusa owns the business data that must be correct for selling products.

  • Products, variants, prices, regions, promotions, cart, checkout, customers, orders, and payments.
  • Custom modules for subscriptions, post-purchase upsells, fulfillment rules, fraud, and warehouse routing.
  • Admin tools for ecommerce managers and customer support workflows.
  • Multiple regions and currencies with independent pricing, not just automatic conversion.
  • Extensible backend workflows for our current order lifecycle and future integrations.

Medusa gives us a commerce backend that can be extended without forcing our business into a fixed SaaS checkout model.

This is important because our current store has custom subscription, upsell, payment, warehouse, tax, and reporting behavior.

For management, Medusa should be seen as the replacement for the ecommerce engine: it is where product availability, pricing, cart rules, checkout rules, customer data, and order behavior live.

Technology 3: Strapi CMS

Strapi is the content management system for the marketing and editorial side of the store.

  • Landing pages.
  • Product page content sections.
  • SEO fields, metadata, FAQs, banners, images, articles, and reusable marketing blocks.
  • Content editing by non-developers.
  • Role-based access for content and marketing teams.
  • Reusable page sections so teams can build pages from approved content blocks.
  • Localization support for translated content and market-specific messaging.
Manager Benefit

Marketing and content teams can update pages without asking developers for every text, image, banner, or landing page change.

Strapi also lets us structure content instead of burying it inside hardcoded templates.

For management, Strapi is the content operations system: it makes the site easier to update while keeping product pricing and checkout logic safely in Medusa.

Supporting Infrastructure

PostgreSQL

Reliable database

Stores commerce and content data. We use separate databases for Medusa and Strapi so each system has clean ownership.

Redis

Cache and jobs

Supports caching and background processing. Helps reduce repeated work during high traffic and keeps public pages faster.

Cloudflare R2

Media storage

Stores images and uploaded assets outside the server, so deployments and scaling do not depend on local upload folders.

Docker

Repeatable deployment

Packages the storefront, commerce, content, database, and cache services so environments are easier to reproduce.

Cloudflare

CDN and protection

Can cache public pages, reduce origin traffic, and provide security protection before requests reach our servers.

Nginx

Traffic routing

Routes public storefront, Medusa admin/API, and Strapi admin/API to the correct service with HTTPS.

Summary: the stack is modular and scalable, not one large application doing everything.Back to TOC

Why Existing Flows Can Be Replicated

Because this is a custom stack, we are not limited by a fixed platform checkout or fixed URL model. In practical terms, the current business flows can be replicated 100% if we build and test them intentionally.

  • Existing URL structure and SEO paths.
  • Product pages and landing pages.
  • Cart drawer and cart page behavior.
  • Checkout fields and payment rules.
  • Post-purchase upsells.
  • Subscriptions and account workflows.
  • Warehouse routing and tracking updates.
  • Loyalty, coupons, store credits, and reporting.
  • Fraud, analytics, CRM, and marketing events.
  • Country, language, and currency behavior.

Traffic And Scaling Assumptions

500/min Regular expected traffic, about 8 requests per second.
1,500-3,000/min TV ad spike range, about 25-50 requests per second.
2 nodes Recommended production app layer for high availability.
HA data Managed database and cache reduce the biggest outage risks.

Public product and landing pages should be aggressively cache-friendly. Cart, checkout, account, and subscription pages should remain dynamic and customer-specific.

Summary: scaling comes from caching public pages, separating data services, and adding app nodes behind a load balancer.Back to TOC

Key Risks And Controls

Risks
  • More engineering ownership than a SaaS platform.
  • Custom flows need complete QA before launch.
  • Production monitoring and backups must be treated as business-critical.
  • Data migration for customers, orders, subscriptions, URLs, and content must be rehearsed.
  • Payment and fulfillment integrations need end-to-end testing.
Controls
  • Use managed database and cache for production.
  • Keep media in R2, not on app servers.
  • Use Cloudflare for CDN, WAF, and traffic offload.
  • Maintain staging environment before every release.
  • Run launch rehearsals and rollback planning.
Summary: the custom path is controllable if operations are planned from the start.Back to TOC
