Executive Summary

Development view: a Shopify migration is possible, but it must be run as a controlled migration project with proof phases before full build. The risky areas are subscriptions, saved payments, URLs, historical data, upsells, and fulfillment logic.

Good Fit

Standard commerce

Products, collections, basic checkout, payments, shipping, discounts, and admin operations can move well to Shopify.

Needs Proof

Custom flows

Subscriptions, post-purchase upsells, fulfillment exports, landing pages, and account behavior need real tests.

Biggest Risk

Revenue continuity

The launch must protect SEO traffic, active subscriptions, payment success, and warehouse processing.

Summary: approve discovery and proof work before approving a full Shopify build.Back to TOC

Short Plan For Meeting

Audit current flows

List products, URLs, subscriptions, customers, orders, upsells, fulfillment, and integrations.

Prove high-risk items

Test subscription migration, saved payments, SEO redirects, upsells, and warehouse export before build.

Choose Shopify model

Decide Shopify plan, theme/headless approach, subscription app, upsell app, and landing page approach.

Build Shopify store

Create storefront, product templates, cart, checkout settings, content templates, and tracking.

Migrate and validate data

Move products, customers, historical orders, subscriptions, SEO data, and redirects with reconciliation reports.

Connect operations

Configure payments, shipping, taxes, reviews, loyalty, email, fraud, support, and fulfillment exports.

Run full QA

Test every purchase path, subscription path, payment method, mobile flow, redirect, and warehouse handoff.

Launch and stabilize

Freeze old site, run final import, switch DNS, monitor 404s, payments, subscriptions, orders, and support tickets.

Meeting summary: Shopify is not just a theme project, it is a data, SEO, subscription, and operations migration.Back to TOC

Phase 1: Discovery And Mapping

Audit
  • Export all URLs and categorize by page type, traffic, revenue, and SEO value.
  • Map products, variants, bundles, kits, landing pages, content pages, and redirects.
  • Document purchase flows, subscription flows, upsells, account actions, refunds, and support actions.
  • List every integration: payments, fraud, warehouse, reviews, loyalty, email, analytics, marketplaces, and support.
Deliverables
  • Current-state flow document.
  • Shopify fit/gap matrix.
  • Full URL and redirect inventory.
  • Data migration inventory.
  • Integration replacement plan.
  • Risk register with owners and test plans.
Summary: this phase prevents surprises by making hidden WooCommerce behavior visible before Shopify build starts.Back to TOC

Phase 2: Risk Validation

This phase should use real sample data, not assumptions. The goal is to prove whether Shopify can support the business-critical flows before we spend heavily on implementation.

Build test store

Create a private Shopify test store with sample products, payment setup, subscription app, upsell app, and app candidates.

Run sample imports

Import representative customers, orders, products, discounts, and subscriptions. Record what works and what needs custom handling.

Test critical flows

Run checkout, subscription purchase, renewal, upsell acceptance, refund, failed payment, and warehouse export scenarios.

Summary: risk validation is the difference between a planned migration and a launch surprise.Back to TOC

SEO And URL Migration

Risk

Shopify has fixed URL patterns for products, collections, blogs, and pages. Existing WooCommerce URLs may not be preserved exactly.

  • Build full redirect map from old URL to Shopify URL.
  • Preserve metadata, titles, descriptions, canonicals, image alt text, and structured data where possible.
  • Check language/country URL behavior and hreflang if applicable.
Acceptance Criteria
  • Top traffic and revenue URLs have approved destination URLs.
  • Redirects work in bulk tests before launch.
  • New sitemaps are clean and submitted after launch.
  • 404 monitoring is active from day one.
  • SEO traffic is monitored daily after launch.
Summary: SEO migration should be treated as a revenue protection workstream.Back to TOC

Customer And Order Data

Tasks
  • Export customers, addresses, tags, marketing consent, account status, and custom fields.
  • Export orders, line items, discounts, refunds, taxes, shipping, payment status, fulfillment status, notes, and metadata.
  • Transform data into Shopify import format and metafields where needed.
  • Create failed-record reports and reconciliation reports.
  • Decide whether all history lives in Shopify or some remains in an archive/reporting system.
Open Questions
  • Can old order IDs be preserved, or do we need a visible legacy order ID field?
  • How will support search old orders after launch?
  • Which custom customer fields must be available in Shopify admin?
  • Which records are required for accounting, legal, and customer service?
Summary: data migration is not only import count, it must preserve support, reporting, and operational usability.Back to TOC

Subscriptions And Saved Payments

Highest Risk

Active subscriptions depend on product mapping, renewal schedules, prices, discounts, customer records, and saved payment tokens. Not all saved payment details can always be transferred into Shopify.

  • Choose subscription app and confirm migration process.
  • Run sample migration with real subscription shapes.
  • Validate renewals, skipped orders, failed payments, cancellations, and discount behavior.
  • Prepare customer reauthorization flow if saved payment details cannot move.
Acceptance Criteria
  • Subscription counts reconcile before and after import.
  • Next billing dates are correct.
  • Recurring prices and discounts are correct.
  • Customers can manage subscriptions after launch.
  • Support has a clear process for failed or unmigrated subscriptions.
Summary: subscriptions should be proven before the migration is approved as feasible.Back to TOC

Custom Development

Likely Custom Work
  • Custom fulfillment export logic if no app matches current rules.
  • Custom order/customer tagging and reporting fields.
  • Custom data migration scripts and validation reports.
  • Custom landing page sections if page-builder output is too limited or slow.
  • Custom app proxy or middleware for workflows Shopify apps cannot cover.
Not Safe To Assume
  • That a Shopify app can copy every current flow exactly.
  • That post-purchase offers work with every payment method and subscription case.
  • That order history imports will look native to support staff.
  • That saved payment methods can always be transferred without customer action.
Summary: custom development fills Shopify gaps, but each gap adds cost, testing, and maintenance.Back to TOC

Launch Runbook

Pre-launch freeze

Define cutoff for content, products, orders, and subscription changes.

Final data sync

Run final exports/imports and reconcile products, customers, orders, and subscriptions.

Go/no-go checks

Validate checkout, payments, redirects, warehouse export, analytics, and support access.

DNS switch

Point production traffic to Shopify and confirm SSL, domain, sitemap, and redirects.

Live order watch

Monitor real orders, payment status, fraud status, fulfillment export, and customer issues.

Rollback decision

Define the point where issues require rollback or hotfix instead of continuing.

Summary: launch must be a rehearsed checklist with owners, not an ad hoc DNS switch.Back to TOC

Post-Launch Stabilization

First 72 Hours
  • Monitor checkout errors, payment failures, subscription issues, warehouse export failures, and 404s.
  • Review customer support tickets several times per day.
  • Fix critical data, redirect, payment, and fulfillment issues immediately.
  • Compare order volume and conversion rate against normal baseline.
First 30 Days
  • Review organic traffic, conversion rate, page speed, subscription churn, upsell revenue, and support volume.
  • Improve redirects and landing pages based on Search Console and analytics.
  • Clean up failed imports and edge cases.
  • Decide what additional custom Shopify development is needed.
Summary: migration is not finished at DNS switch, it needs active stabilization after launch.Back to TOC

Key Risks To Present

Main Risks
  • SEO loss from changed URLs or incomplete redirects.
  • Subscription disruption if payment details cannot migrate cleanly.
  • Historical order data may not look or behave exactly like native Shopify orders.
  • Post-purchase and cart upsells may be limited by apps or payment methods.
  • Fulfillment export logic may require custom development.
  • Too many apps can slow the storefront and create maintenance risk.
Controls
  • Run proof-of-risk phase before full approval.
  • Use real sample data for migration testing.
  • Build a full redirect map and monitor 404s after launch.
  • Prepare customer communication for password or payment reauthorization.
  • Run launch rehearsal and keep rollback decision criteria clear.
  • Track conversion, speed, subscriptions, and support tickets after launch.
Summary: Shopify can be considered, but the risky flows must be proven before the business commits.Back to TOC
