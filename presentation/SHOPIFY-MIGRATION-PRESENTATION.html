Executive Summary

Recommendation: treat Shopify as a possible path only if we are willing to simplify parts of the current customer journey. Shopify can help with platform stability, but it creates serious migration risk around SEO, subscriptions, post-purchase upsells, and custom fulfillment flows.

Shopify Strength

Operational simplicity

Shopify can reduce hosting, security, infrastructure, and day-to-day platform maintenance work.

Main Concern

Business flow mismatch

Our current site has custom subscriptions, upsells, landing pages, warehouse routing, SEO paths, and reporting flows.

Key Risk

Revenue disruption

The largest risks are SEO loss, subscription migration friction, and changes to post-purchase upsell behavior.

Summary: Shopify is strong, but it works best when the business can adapt to Shopify’s model.Back to TOC

What We Are Trying To Achieve

The goal is not just to rebuild the website. The goal is to upgrade the ecommerce platform so it can support growth.

  • Faster page loads and smoother customer flow.
  • Better stability during traffic spikes and campaigns.
  • Reduced checkout friction and fewer technical slowdowns.
  • Preserved SEO value from existing pages and landing pages.
  • Reliable subscriptions, upsells, fulfillment, analytics, and support workflows.

The Shopify decision is about tradeoffs

What Shopify improves: platform stability, managed infrastructure, standard ecommerce operations, and less maintenance burden.

What Shopify risks: less control over URLs, checkout behavior, subscriptions, upsells, and custom operational flows.

Decision lens: speed and scale matter, but revenue continuity matters more.Back to TOC

What Shopify Would Be Good For

Pros
  • Less responsibility for hosting, security patches, and core ecommerce uptime.
  • Strong admin experience for standard ecommerce operations.
  • Large app ecosystem for common needs: reviews, email, loyalty, analytics, shipping, and payments.
  • Stable checkout and payment infrastructure.
  • Built-in scaling for normal ecommerce traffic growth.
Best Fit

Shopify is a good fit if we are willing to simplify the current store and use more standard ecommerce flows.

It is less ideal if we need to preserve every custom path, landing page, upsell, subscription rule, and warehouse process exactly.

Summary: Shopify lowers platform maintenance, but it also reduces flexibility.Back to TOC

What Shopify Can Migrate Relatively Easily

Basic ecommerce data can move. The hard part is not the catalog. The hard part is preserving the custom business engine around the catalog.

  • Products, titles, descriptions, images, SKUs.
  • Simple product variants.
  • Basic collections.
  • Standard content pages.
  • Basic promotions and discount codes.
  • Simple customer profiles.
  • Basic reviews, email, loyalty, and support app integrations.
  • Basic multilingual and multicurrency setup through Shopify Markets.
Summary: the simple store layer is portable; the custom revenue flows are not simple.Back to TOC

Top Problem 1: SEO And URL Structure

Shopify does not allow full control over core URL structure. Products, collections, pages, and blogs must follow Shopify’s fixed patterns.

  • Current site has custom product paths, language/country paths, landing page paths, and SEO history.
  • Shopify can redirect old URLs, but redirects are not the same as preserving the exact original URLs.
  • Organic traffic, backlinks, paid campaign landing pages, and indexed pages all need a careful migration map.

Business impact

Potential SEO ranking loss during and after launch, especially for pages that currently bring organic traffic or are used in campaigns.

Risk level: high.

Summary: this is one of the strongest arguments against a native Shopify migration.Back to TOC

Top Problem 2: Existing Orders, Customers, And Subscriptions

Data Area What Can Move Main Risk
Customers Names, emails, addresses, tags, marketing consent if mapped correctly. Passwords usually cannot move directly. Customers may need account activation. Duplicates and consent rules need cleanup.
Orders Historical orders can be imported for reference and support. Old orders may not behave like native Shopify orders for refunds, tracking, tax, fraud, chargebacks, and reporting.
Subscriptions Products, renewal dates, shipping details, and subscription records may be imported. Saved payment methods may not migrate cleanly. Some customers may need to re-enter payment details before renewal.
Summary: subscription migration is the highest-risk data workstream.Back to TOC

Why Saved Payment Details Are A Problem

For subscriptions, the platform needs a saved payment method so it can charge the customer again on renewal.

We do not store full card numbers. Payment providers store secure tokens. Those tokens are often tied to the current payment setup and may not work automatically inside Shopify.

Possible outcome

  • Customer and subscription data migrate.
  • Renewal dates migrate.
  • But some saved payment methods fail to migrate.
  • Those customers need to add payment details again.

Business risk: failed renewals, customer support load, and subscription churn if the transition is not handled carefully.

Summary: this needs a test migration before any Shopify commitment.Back to TOC

Top Problem 3: Custom Checkout, Upsells, And Fulfillment

Checkout

Current checkout has custom fields, payment rules, account behavior, address behavior, and marketing consent.

Shopify checkout is stable, but less flexible. Deep changes often require Shopify Plus.

Upsells

Current post-purchase upsell flow can charge the customer after purchase and update the order/subscription.

Shopify post-purchase offers have payment, currency, and checkout restrictions.

Fulfillment

Current warehouse flow includes custom order routing, address validation, shipment updates, and tracking behavior.

This would need a custom Shopify-compatible workflow.

Summary: Shopify apps can cover standard cases, but our current flow is not standard.Back to TOC

What Cannot Be Fully Replicated On Shopify

Current Capability Shopify Reality Expected Compromise
Exact URL preservation Shopify uses fixed URL patterns. Redirects and SEO monitoring instead of exact path preservation.
Exact checkout behavior Shopify checkout is controlled and limited. Use apps/extensions and possibly Shopify Plus, but accept some differences.
Exact post-purchase upsell flow Shopify post-purchase offers do not run for every payment or order type. Lower control and possible upsell coverage gaps.
Exact subscription portal and renewal rules Subscription providers have their own models. Rebuild and QA the customer portal, dunning, renewals, and payment migration.
Summary: Shopify can approximate many flows, but not copy them exactly.Back to TOC

Shopify Decision Conditions

Question If The Answer Is Yes If The Answer Is No
Can we accept URL changes and rely on redirects? Shopify remains possible, with a strong SEO migration plan. Shopify becomes high risk for organic traffic and campaign landing pages.
Can we simplify the checkout and post-purchase flow? Shopify apps and Plus features may be enough. Shopify will likely force revenue-impacting compromises.
Can active subscriptions migrate without major payment failures? Shopify can be considered after a successful test migration. Shopify creates churn risk and support load.
Can our warehouse and reporting flows be rebuilt with Shopify apps or middleware? Shopify can support operations with custom setup. Daily operations may become less reliable after launch.
Are we willing to use Shopify Plus if needed? More checkout and post-purchase options become available. Some important customizations may be unavailable.
Summary: Shopify should be approved only if these conditions are acceptable.Back to TOC

Shopify Recommendation

Recommendation: do not approve a Shopify migration yet as a direct replacement for the current site.

Shopify should move forward only after proof that it can handle the highest-risk areas:

  • SEO URL migration without unacceptable traffic loss.
  • Active subscription migration without major payment failures.
  • Post-purchase upsells with acceptable coverage and conversion impact.
  • Warehouse routing and reporting that match daily operational needs.

When Shopify makes sense

Shopify becomes more attractive if management wants to reduce platform maintenance and is comfortable simplifying parts of the current ecommerce flow.

That means accepting URL changes, checkout limitations, app dependency, and a dedicated subscription migration project.

Summary: Shopify is not a safe direct copy of the current business without validation.Back to TOC

Suggested Next Steps

1

SEO inventory

Create a full list of current URLs, high-traffic pages, landing pages, backlinks, and campaign URLs. Score what Shopify cannot preserve.

2

Subscription test migration

Run a small proof of concept with real subscription scenarios, saved payment methods, renewal dates, and account management.

3

Flow prototype

Test one post-purchase upsell, one warehouse route, one loyalty/coupon case, and one multilingual landing page before making a final Shopify decision.

Decision gate: if Shopify cannot pass these tests without major revenue or SEO compromises, we should not proceed with Shopify as the migration path.

Summary: prove the risky flows before committing to a platform change.Back to TOC
